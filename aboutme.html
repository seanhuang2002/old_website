About Me

Who am I?

My name is Sean Huang and I am a 17 year old Computer Science Major at the University of Waterloo in the Faculty of Mathematics. I was born and raised in Toronto and I have a passion for computer science, history, politics and aviation. I am a graduate of the Toronto French School's International Baccalaureate Diploma Programme with an Advanced Bilingual Diploma.

My Skills

Through the various computer science courses I have taken and the various computer science related programmes I have attended, I have gained a wide variety of skills such as an intimate knowledge of Java and its application framework JavaFX, SQL and an elemental grasp of HTML and XML. I also know how to utilize blockchain technology in various applications.

My Background

I have always been interested in computer science and topics related to technology. Much of this was gained during my primary and secondary education. Most of my schooling occured at the Toronto French School, a renowned and well respected French independent school in Northern Toronto. I participated in the rigourous International Baccalaureate Diploma programme where I took Chemistry, English Literature and History in the higher level, along with Physics, Mathematics and French in standard level. In addition to the rigourous IBDP, I opted to enroll in an optional Computer Science course, where I gained all my knowledge in computer science and programming. I have participated in the University of Waterloo's Canadian Computing Contest at both the Junior and Senior levels.

My Interests

Technology

I have a great passion about computer science and technology, its why I'm currently studying it!
One of my goals is, quite simply, to learn more about how these complex machines, and the software they run, function.

While my primary interest is in the software side of computers, the hardware side hasn't failed to rub off on me either!
